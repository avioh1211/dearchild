Dear

A portrait project about the message only you can send — from who you are now, to who you once were.

The project

A message from the present
to the child you were

Dear Child is a portrait series built around a simple idea: that the adult you are now has something to say to the child you once were. Not a question. Not advice. A message. Something that child needed to hear. Something only you can say.

Each participant chooses an age from their past — the one that arrives, not the one they think they should choose. From that place, if something wants to be said, they write it down. Then I photograph them. Two portraits: one before, one after. The face going in, and the face that returns.

The written message is displayed alongside the portraits in the exhibition — in a small glass frame, in your own handwriting, between the two photographs of your face.

The session

What will
happen

Here is the shape of our time together, so you can arrive without needing to prepare anything.

01

We meet

We start slowly. A cup of tea or water, wherever we are. No agenda. Just arriving together. We'll also talk about the location — where feels right for you. We find it together.

02

A short meditation

I'll guide you through a short meditation. You don't need to prepare for it or know what's coming. Just arrive.

03

The writing

If something wants to be said to that child — a word, a feeling, a message — you write it down by hand, in the space, in the moment. As much or as little as arrives. It can also be nothing. This is yours.

04

The portrait

When you look up, I'll be there. You don't need to pose. You don't need to perform. Just stay with whatever is still there. I'll come to you. Two photographs — one with your eyes closed, one open.

One more thing

A gentle
note

As the photographer, I find that clothing can sometimes draw attention away from what the portrait is really about — the face, the body, the moment itself.

If it feels right to you, you're welcome to remove your upper clothing for the portrait. Not for how it looks — but to let the image breathe, and let you be seen more simply. As the photographer, I sometimes wonder if there's something true about meeting your younger self without the layers we've learned to wear.

If it doesn't feel right — your portrait is complete exactly as you are. There is no preference, no pressure, no right answer. You don't need to decide anything now. We can talk about it when we meet, or not at all.

Practical

Before
we meet

A few simple things:

  • Wear something you feel like yourself in. There's no dress code.
  • There's nothing to rehearse or figure out in advance. You don't need to know your age or your message before you arrive.
  • If an age or a feeling surfaces before we meet — let it. But don't force it.
  • The session takes roughly one to two hours including finding our location together.
  • Your written text will be displayed in the exhibition alongside your portraits, in your own handwriting. We'll discuss this together and you'll have full say over what is shown.
  • All images are black and white. You will see and approve the final selections before anything is printed or exhibited.
